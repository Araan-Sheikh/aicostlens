# GTM

## Target User

The exact user is a founder, CTO, VP Engineering, or engineering manager at a seed to Series B startup with 5-75 employees, at least one developer-heavy workflow, and more than one paid AI subscription. They are not buying an enterprise procurement platform. They are usually the person who approved Cursor, Copilot, ChatGPT Team, Claude, or API usage because the team needed to ship faster, and now the spend is spread across cards, invoices, and individual seats.

## Trigger Moment

They want this tool right after seeing an unexpectedly high OpenAI, Anthropic, Cursor, or ChatGPT invoice, or when finance asks why the company has five overlapping AI tools. The searches are concrete: "Cursor business pricing vs pro", "ChatGPT team worth it", "reduce OpenAI bill startup", "AI tool spend audit", and "Claude API cost too high". On social feeds, the trigger is a founder post about burn, runway, Cursor bills, or switching coding assistants.

## Where They Hang Out

The strongest channels are founder and builder communities where spend screenshots and tool debates already happen: X accounts/lists around indie hackers and AI tooling, Hacker News launch and Show HN threads, r/SaaS, r/startups, r/LocalLLaMA for model cost discussions, Indie Hackers, YC Bookface-style founder groups, and Slack/Discord spaces such as build-in-public groups, college founder clubs, and startup operator communities. The wedge is not broad SEO; it is entering active conversations where people are already comparing AI tools.

## First 100 Users With $0 Paid Budget

First, publish a short "AI stack waste calculator" launch post with screenshots of the audit output, not a generic landing page. Post it on X, Hacker News Show HN, r/SaaS, r/startups, and Indie Hackers with a specific hook: "Paste your AI stack, see if your team is double-paying." Second, DM 50 founders/CTOs who recently posted about Cursor, Copilot, Claude, or OpenAI bills and offer to run their stack through the tool manually if they do not want to type it in. Third, ask every interview respondent and early user for one anonymized public report that can be shared as proof. Fourth, write a teardown thread comparing three common stacks: "2-person team", "10-person dev team", and "API-heavy AI app".

## Unfair Distribution Channel

Credex has a unique wedge: it can identify real savings and then offer discounted AI infrastructure credits as the next step. A normal calculator can say "you might save money"; Credex can say "here is the path to capture the saving without changing workflow." The best distribution channel is therefore a Credex-owned weekly teardown of anonymized AI spend stacks from real startups.

## Week 1 Traction Goal

If this works, week 1 should produce 100 completed audits, 35 saved reports, 10 high-savings reports above $500/month, 5 booked consultations, and at least 3 anonymized reports that can be used as social proof. The most important signal is not traffic. It is whether serious operators finish the audit and say the recommendation is specific enough to act on.
