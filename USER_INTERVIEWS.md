# User Interviews

## Interview 1 — Shivang Kumar Sinha

**Role:** Chief Growth Officer
**Company:** Elitro Technologies  
**Company stage:** TODO: confirm stage  
**Date:** 2026-05-22  
**Format:** Text response

### Context

Shivang responded to the AI spend audit interview questions over text. His team currently pays for Claude and OpenAI, has meaningful AI spend, and would consider booking a call if the audit found a large enough savings opportunity.

### Quotes

- "we have subscriptions for claude, openai"
- "no on tracks any, we just ensure it does not go over budget unless its needed to"
- "we spend around $8000"
- "we would trust it if in any manner it helps reduce the costs keeping the productivity as is"
- "i suppose alternative vendor or a consolidation"
- "yes"

### Most surprising thing

The most surprising signal was that the team spends around $8000 while still saying no one actively tracks AI subscriptions closely. That supports the core problem in the assignment: teams may have meaningful AI spend without a clear benchmark, owner, or regular review process.

### What changed in my design

This pushed the product toward recommendations that preserve productivity instead of simply telling users to cut tools. The audit should make consolidation and alternative-vendor recommendations very explicit, because that is what this respondent said they would trust. It also suggests public sharing should be optional and privacy-first, because they answered "no" to sharing a public savings report.
