## Interview 1 — Shivang Kumar Sinha

**Role:** Chief Growth Officer
**Company:** Elitro Technologies  
**Company stage:** TODO: confirm stage  
**Date:** 2026-05-22  
**Format:** Text response

### Context

Shivang responded to the AI spend audit interview questions over text. His team currently pays for Claude and OpenAI, has meaningful AI spend, and would consider booking a call if the audit found a large enough savings opportunity.

### Quotes

- "we have subscriptions for claude, openai"
- "no on tracks any, we just ensure it does not go over budget unless its needed to"
- "we spend around $8000"
- "we would trust it if in any manner it helps reduce the costs keeping the productivity as is"
- "i suppose alternative vendor or a consolidation"
- "yes"

### Most surprising thing

The most surprising signal was that the team spends around $8000 while still saying no one actively tracks AI subscriptions closely. That supports the core problem in the assignment: teams may have meaningful AI spend without a clear benchmark, owner, or regular review process.

### What changed in my design

This pushed the product toward recommendations that preserve productivity instead of simply telling users to cut tools. The audit should make consolidation and alternative-vendor recommendations very explicit, because that is what this respondent said they would trust. It also suggests public sharing should be optional and privacy-first, because they answered "no" to sharing a public savings report.

## Interview 2 — Alyaz

**Role:** CTO  
**Company:** TUMULL.VAULT  
**Company stage:** Early-stage
**Date:** 2026-05-23  
**Format:** Text response

### Context

Alyaz responded as CTO of TUMULL.VAULT, an early-stage startup using a broad AI stack for development, research, content, automation, and product experiments. Their current AI spend is lower than Shivang's team, but the overlap across ChatGPT, Copilot, Cursor, Claude, Gemini, and APIs makes consolidation a clear concern.

### Quotes

- "We currently use ChatGPT, GitHub Copilot, Cursor, Claude, Gemini, and some API-based AI services for development, research, content, automation, and product experiments."
- "As CTO, I usually evaluate the technical value, usage, and productivity impact."
- "Our current AI spend is around $150-$300/month, depending on API testing, subscriptions, and active development work."
- "The recommendation should explain the reasoning, not just say 'cancel this.'"
- "For us, tool consolidation and seat cleanup would be the most useful."
- "Many AI tools overlap, so knowing which tools can replace others without reducing productivity would help a lot."
- "Yes, if company name, emails, employee names, and sensitive billing details are hidden, we would be open to sharing an anonymized savings report."
- "If the audit clearly shows $500+/month in possible savings with low risk, I would be interested in a short call to understand the recommendations better."

### Most surprising thing

The most surprising thing was that even a lean pre-seed team already uses a wide set of overlapping AI tools. Their spend is not huge yet, but the workflow overlap is real. This suggests the product should not only target high-spend companies; it should also help early teams avoid messy AI stacks before the monthly bill becomes painful.

### What changed in my design

This reinforced the need for clear reasoning behind every recommendation. The audit should not simply say "cancel this"; it should explain the productivity trade-off, overlap, seat usage, and why a consolidation path is low risk. It also supports keeping anonymized public reports, because Alyaz would share a report if sensitive company and billing details were hidden.
