# Economics

This product works if it turns anonymous AI spend pain into qualified Credex conversations at a lower cost than outbound sales. The rough model below uses conservative assumptions because the assignment does not provide Credex pricing or margin data.

Assume a startup that buys discounted AI credits spends $2,000-$10,000/month on eligible AI infrastructure or tools. If Credex earns the equivalent of 8%-15% gross margin on credits, a converted customer could be worth $160-$1,500/month in gross profit. Using a midpoint of $5,000/month credit volume and 10% gross margin, one converted lead is worth about $500/month, or $6,000/year before churn. If average retention is one year, a qualified purchase is worth roughly $6,000 gross profit. If retention is eighteen months, it is closer to $9,000.

Channel CAC should be low at the start because the product itself is the hook. For founder DMs, the cash CAC is $0 but time cost is real: if 50 DMs take 4 hours and create 10 audits, 4 saved leads, and 1 booked call, then the early CAC per booked call is mainly founder time. For Show HN, Reddit, and Indie Hackers launches, cash CAC is also $0, but the conversion will be volatile. A reasonable first-month target is 1,000 visitors, 200 completed audits, 70 saved reports, 20 high-savings reports, 8 booked consultations, and 2 purchases.

At those rates, the funnel is: 20% visitor to completed audit, 35% completed audit to email capture, 28% saved report to high-savings qualified lead, 40% high-savings lead to consultation, and 25% consultation to credit purchase. With 2 purchases at $6,000 annual gross profit each, the product creates about $12,000 in annual gross profit from a $0 paid launch. Even if engineering and founder time are counted, that is promising.

For the tool to drive $1M ARR in 18 months, Credex needs approximately $1M / $6,000 = 167 retained credit customers at the midpoint. If the completed audit to purchase rate is 1%, the product needs about 16,700 completed audits over 18 months, or about 930 completed audits/month. That is high but plausible if the audit becomes a repeatable lead magnet through launch posts, public report sharing, partner newsletters, and Credex sales outreach.

The economics break if high-savings reports are rare or if recommendations feel untrustworthy. A pivot would be needed if fewer than 5% of completed audits show $500+/month in credible savings, or if consultation-to-purchase conversion stays below 10%.
